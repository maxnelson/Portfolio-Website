My time at Rubrik was brief but action-packed, I came on at a time when the company was growing quickly, and during the year I spent there a lot of progress was made developing their online presence. The two most notable achievements being:

  1. Migrated the rubrik.com website from one hosting provider / CDN (Cloudflare) to another (Akamai).
  2. Migrated the rubrik.com web platform / CMS from Wordpress to Adobe Experience Manage (AEM).

Prior to both of these migrations, I was hired on as the companies only in-house Web Developer, situated underneath the Marketing department.

This meant working closely with the agency that Rubrik used to develop and maintain their Marketing website. This agency called Pixelmatters, is located in Portugal, which meant a lot of early morning calls:), however they are highly skilled, and were a pleasure to work with.

Working with Pixelmatters, I developed Wordpress page templates and components, using PHP (Timber, Twig), HTML, CSS (BEM), and Javascript. I also made global style adjustments and fixed small functionality bugs in various website components.

Midway into the year, we began the migration from Wordpress to AEM. For the AEM site, Rubrik employed another agency, Qualified Digital to build and deploy the site.

As the only in-house Web Developer, I worked closely with Qualified Digital to translate the designs (generated by a tertiary agency), into functioning web components. Midway through this process we hired on an in-house back-end AEM developer, although he was situated offshore in India.

During the migration I wore many hats, and assisted the Marketing department in a handful of projects, including the following:

  • Administered the Google Analytics and Google Tag Manager accounts, configuring events, publishing triggers, and generating reports for website stakeholders.
  • Conducted weekly website performance tests, identifying and implementing strategies / processes for optimization.
  • Used tools like the AEM CMS' Bulk Editor and VLT-RPC tools to import manage, sync and replicate content (marketing copy, image assets) across AEM instances (dev, stage, prod, pub).
  • Used CRXDELite to troubleshoot content issues
  • Assisted in managing a CDN migration, working with Cloudflare and later Akamai to implement proper DNS configurations on existing, as well as newly acquired web domains.
  • Developed the frontend of many AEM components, using Javascript, HTML, CSS, HTL (Sightly), and Apache Sling to integrate with OSGI-registered Java servlet applications, and a JCR Jackrabbit Oak repository.
  • Developed a variety of components, including everything from Page section components to dynamic web forms with strict frontend validation. For example, developed a Frontend integration with the Greenhouse API; used data to develop the careers listing, careers detail, and Job application form components.

After the site launched successfully, I was sadly let go from the company, due to disagreements between myself and my manager. However, it was a great experience and I have fond memories of my time, co-workers, and experiences at Rubrik.

Technologies Used: Docker, Wordpress, PHP, Timber, Twig, Javascript, Node, HTML, CSS (BEM), AEM, Git
